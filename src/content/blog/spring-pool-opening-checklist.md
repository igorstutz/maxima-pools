The first warm weekend always brings the same call: *"How do I open the pool?"* Whether you're DIY-ing it or having us handle it, the steps are the same — and skipping any one of them costs you weeks of swim time.

## 1. Don't Pump the Cover First

The water sitting on top of your winter cover is full of leaves, runoff, and whatever blew in over five months. Pumping it before you remove the debris on top sends the dirty water straight into the pool. Skim what you can off the top first, then pump.

## 2. Inspect Before You Refill

Before water comes back up to the skimmer line, walk the edges. You're looking for:

- Cracks or stress marks in the deck (rare on fiberglass, but worth a glance)
- Damaged tile, loose grout
- Skimmer baskets, pump lids, drain covers — all in place

Catching a hairline crack now is a cheap fix. Catching it in July is a half-summer pause.

## 3. Reinstall, Then Prime

Reattach drain plugs, return jets, and the pump basket. Fill the pool above the skimmer halfway, **then** prime the pump. Running a dry pump for thirty seconds is enough to score a seal. Don't.

## 4. Test Before You Shock

Test water — pH, alkalinity, calcium hardness, chlorine. Adjust alkalinity first (80–120 ppm), pH next (7.4–7.6), then calcium. **Then** shock. Shocking unbalanced water is how you end up with cloudy green for a week.

## 5. Run the Filter for 24 Hours

After shock, the filter pulls out the particulates that just got killed. Don't go swimming yet. Test again the next morning. If chlorine is back to 1–3 ppm and the water is clear, you're swimming.
