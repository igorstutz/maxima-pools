If you've spent any time researching backyard pools in Columbus, you've heard the same three options thrown around: concrete (gunite), vinyl liner, and fiberglass. They're not equal. After installing pools across Central Ohio for years, we've watched freeze-thaw cycles tear up concrete shells and split vinyl liners — while our fiberglass installations keep going strong.

## Built for the Way Ohio Winters Move

Ohio soil doesn't sit still. Ground freezes, expands, thaws, contracts — repeat. A rigid concrete shell has nowhere to go, so the pressure shows up as cracks, hairlines that grow, and eventually the kind of repair bill that makes you wish you'd chosen differently.

Fiberglass shells are engineered to flex with that movement. The same material technology used in marine hulls and aerospace components. A pool that bends slightly with the ground — and snaps back — instead of fighting it.

## Lower Lifetime Cost — Not Just Lower Maintenance

The sticker price story isn't fiberglass's strongest pitch. The **decade-by-decade** story is.

- **No re-plastering.** Concrete needs resurfacing every 10–15 years. Fiberglass doesn't.
- **No liner replacement.** Vinyl tears and ages out every 7–10 years. Fiberglass shells outlast the house.
- **Fewer chemicals.** A non-porous gel coat means algae has nothing to grab onto. Less chlorine, less labor.
- **Faster install.** 3–5 days from delivery to swimming, vs. weeks of cure time for concrete.

Add it up across 20 years and the math flips fast.

## What to Look For

Not all fiberglass pools are equal — and the difference is in the laminate, not the look.

1. **Hand-laid woven roving** instead of chopped-gun spray. Continuous fibers, uniform thickness, no thin spots.
2. **Vinyl Ester resin** throughout the shell, not just the outer gel coat. The same chemical family as marine epoxy.
3. **Structural ribs** that reinforce the walls without adding deadweight.
4. **A real warranty.** Not just "structural" — read what's actually covered.
